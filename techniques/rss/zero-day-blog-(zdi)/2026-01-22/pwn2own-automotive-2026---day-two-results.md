Day Two of Pwn2Own Automotive 2026 was packed with action, and the stakes continued to rise. Security researchers returned to the Pwn2Own stage, probing and challenging the latest automotive systems as the competition intensified. New exploits, unexpected twists, and standout performances emerged throughout the day - follow along here for daily updates as the race for Master of Pwn heats up.&nbsp;  Following an action-packed Day One, where $516,500 USD&nbsp;was awarded for 37&nbsp;unique 0-day vulnerabilities, Day Two added another $439,250 USD and 29 unique 0-days, bringing the event totals to $955,750 USD with 66 unique vulnerabilities overall. Fuzzware.io holds a commanding lead for Master of Pwn, but with one day to go, anything can still happen. We’ll see what the final day of the contest brings.&nbsp;
