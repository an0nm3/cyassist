 Imagine you&#8217;re on a physical engagement, standing outside an office door. You need an access card but you don&#8217;t have one (yet). You notice that there&#8217;s a pattern where employees need to tag in, but to leave they just wave their hand and the door swings open. You pull a torch out of your backpack and switch it on. There&#8217;s no visible light but a subtle vibration assures you that it&#8217;s on and working. You shine it through the glass door, pointing it at a bookshelf, a chair or wall on the inside, like trying to line up a shot in pool. Within about 5 seconds&#8230; pop! The door swings open, there&#8217;s nobody else in sight and you walk right in. Not even a fingerprint left behind. It turns out, this scenario isn&#8217;t as farfetched as you might think.
