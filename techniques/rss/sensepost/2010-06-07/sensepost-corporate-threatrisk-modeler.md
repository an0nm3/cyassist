Since joining SensePost I&#8217;ve had a chance to get down and dirty with the threat modeling tool. The original principle behind the tool, first released in  2007 at CSI NetSec , was to throw out existing threat modeling techniques (it&#8217;s really attack-focused risk) and start from scratch. It&#8217;s a good idea and the SensePost approach fits nicely between the heavily formalised models like Octave and the quick-n-dirty&#8217;s like attack trees. It allows fairly simple modeling of the organisation/system to quickly produce an exponentially larger list of possible risks and rank them.
