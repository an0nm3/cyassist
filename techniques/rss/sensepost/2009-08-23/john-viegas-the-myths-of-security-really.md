i go through a ton of books. Over the past 10 years, this has been dominated by books on computer security, computer science, programming (and some sprinklings of management classics). 
 I generally stay away from writing reviews, but was genuinely suprised at the number of 5 star reviews Viega&#8217;s new book had received and felt i had to chime in. 
 I picked up &#8220;the myths of security&#8221; (what the computer industry doesn&#8217;t want you to know) with hope, because O&#8217;Reilly books in general are well done and i really liked some of Johns previous books. Alas! I tried hard to think of a good thing to say about the book, and the best i can come up with right now is that &#8220;at least, it wont take up space on my bookshelf&#8221;. 
    
The book is tiny (48 chapters, where each chapter is between a paragraph to 2-3 pages) which isn&#8217;t a bad thing, but it reads mostly as a collection of blog posts or hurriedly written notes-to-self.
