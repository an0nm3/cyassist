Rural commerce startup Wheelocity has raised over ₹82 Cr (about $8.5 Mn) in an ongoing funding round from new investors, existing backers, and a host of angel investors. Overall, the startup has raised ₹82.36 Cr across three tranches, as per its MCA filings. While it secured ₹16.32 Cr from Lightspeed India Partners III LLC, LS Opportunities Access Fund LP, Grand Anicut Fund 3 and Magnum LLC, it raised ₹11.05 Cr from VML Karthikeyan, Lakshmi Narayanan Senthilnathan, V.M. Lakshminarayanan, L. Gurusami and Unimix LLC in March via two separate transactions. The latest tranche in June brought in ₹54.99 Cr from EE-FI (Elevar Equity) AIF, represented by its trustee Vistra ITCL (India) Limited. Wheelocity founder and CEO Selvam VMS declined to comment on Inc42’s queries on the funding, saying the round is still ongoing.
