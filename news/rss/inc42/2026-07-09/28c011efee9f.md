When BluSmart’s cabs suddenly disappeared from the roads of Delhi NCR last year, the region&#8217;s ride-hailing market — after years of being a duopoly and briefly seeing meaningful competition — was effectively reset to square one. In a market dominated by Rapido and Uber, BluSmart earned a loyal customer base by combining competitive pricing with cleaner cabs, reliable service, zero cancellations, and courteous drivers. The abrupt exit of the EV ride-hailing service left commuters with fewer alternatives, exposing them once again to surge pricing, inconsistent service, and poor ride quality that have long defined India&#8217;s ride-hailing market. On the other hand, however, BluSmart’s screeching halt left a white space. Observing the market closely was Vietnamese electric mobility company Green SM, which leapt in to fill it.
