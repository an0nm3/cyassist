Karnataka has thrown down a marker for the next chapter of India's technology story. Chief Minister D.K. Shivakumar on Tuesday brought more than 150 global business leaders under one roof in Bengaluru and made the State's ambition unmistakable: 500 new Global Capability Centres, 3.5 lakh high-quality jobs, and US$50 billion in economic output by 2029.The occasion was KATALYST CONNECT, the Chief Minister's meet with Global Capability Centre (GCC) leaders, hosted by the Department of Electronics, Information Technology and Biotechnology in partnership with the Karnataka Digital Economy Mission (KDEM).
